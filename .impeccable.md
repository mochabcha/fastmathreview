## Design Context

### Users
Upper-elementary students, families, tutors, and teachers using a tablet or desktop to practice Florida 4th grade FAST math benchmarks. The job is to move between low-pressure review and test-like practice without losing sight of the standard being learned.

### Brand Personality
Clear, steady, encouraging. The interface should feel academically credible without looking sterile, and energetic without becoming game-noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a clean hybrid of testing-tool focus and bright practice-tool warmth. Favor strong reading clarity, visible progress, structured panels, and math-forward typography. Avoid cartoon-heavy visuals, dark intimidating exam vibes, and generic SaaS dashboards.

### Design Principles
1. Reading comes first: question text and inline math must stay highly legible on tablets and desktops.
2. Standards stay visible: benchmark, reference help, and review guidance should never feel buried.
3. Modes must behave differently: testing should reduce hints, while review should teach immediately and precisely.
4. Tokens over one-offs: spacing, color, widths, and surfaces should come from the shared theme layer.
5. Expansion-ready content: question data, standards metadata, and instructional links must stay JSON-driven and replaceable.
